Workforce practices vs business outcomes

Measure how people decisions shape KPI movement and revenue over time

A multi-year, time-aware modeling pipeline that connects employee performance and compensation practices to workforce stability, KPI changes, and next-period revenue. Built for decision support, not vibes.

What this answers

Leaders make performance decisions constantly: promotions, merit, recognition, development, and improvement actions. Impact Audit asks whether those practices are producing the workforce the business needs today and what that implies in dollars.

Time-aware panel modeling Effective-dated joins Stay model → attrition risk Two-stage mechanism model Fixed effects (BU + month) Model-based what-if impacts

Decision support focus

  • Where is revenue exposed due to workforce instability?
  • Which operational levers move next-period revenue most?
  • How do changes in staffing dynamics translate into future outcomes?
  • Are comp and performance practices aligned with KPI improvements over time?

Core outputs

Attrition risk
Employee-month risk scores using a stay probability model, bucketed into time-to-exit risk.
KPI-next predictions (Stage 1)
Predict next-period KPI movement from workforce and current-state features.
Revenue forecast (Stage 2)
Predict next-period BU revenue from predicted KPI-next plus BU and time fixed effects.
KPI what-if impacts
Dollar deltas from controlled KPI changes using the trained revenue model.
Recent run: revenue model R2 improved after the two-stage + fixed effects upgrade.

Method summary (A + B)

  • Time-aware snapshots: employee-month and BU-month panels that avoid future leakage.
  • Effective-dated joins: groupwise as-of attachment of compensation and performance history.
  • Stay modeling: learn risk signal using time-to-exit buckets; operational label is attrition within a forward horizon.
  • Stage 1 mechanism model: workforce and current KPIs predict next-period KPIs.
  • Stage 2 revenue model: predicted KPI-next plus fixed effects predict next-period revenue.
  • Fixed effects: BU and month effects reduce “some units are always better” bias.
  • What-if analysis: reuse the revenue model to price KPI changes into next-period dollars.

Interpretation guardrails

  • Outputs are decision support, not causal proof.
  • What-if impacts are model-based marginal estimates holding other features constant.
  • Some “improvements” can have short-term costs and long-term payoff.
  • Use lift and concentration for targeting, not accuracy alone.
  • If you want causal inference, you need a real intervention design (policy change, natural experiment, or matched controls).

Outputs and what they mean

  • panel_scored.csv: employee-month panel with features and attrition risk scores.
  • panel_scores_min.csv: minimal scoring output for action lists (employee, period, BU, risk).
  • bu_period_features.csv: aggregated BU-period feature set used for KPI and revenue modeling.
  • bu_period_kpi_scored.csv: Stage 1 outputs including predicted next-period KPIs.
  • bu_period_scored.csv: Stage 2 outputs including predicted next-period revenue.
  • kpi_impact_output.csv: BU-period predicted revenue deltas per KPI change.
  • kpi_impact_summary.csv: summary statistics (mean, p10, p50, p90) per KPI impact, plus totals rows (net and absolute).

Dashboard pages

  • Revenue at risk: risk distribution, risk tiers, BU concentration, intervention list.

Revenue-at-risk calculation

Converts attrition risk into expected revenue exposure at the BU-period level using predicted next-period revenue.

import pandas as pd panel = pd.read_csv("panel_scored.csv") bu = pd.read_csv("bu_period_scored.csv") # Merge BU revenue and headcount onto employees df = panel.merge( bu[["business_unit_id", "period_start", "pred_revenue_next", "headcount"]], on=["business_unit_id", "period_start"], how="left" ) # Per-employee revenue exposure (simple approximation) df["revenue_exposure"] = df["pred_revenue_next"] / df["headcount"] # Expected revenue at risk per employee df["revenue_risk"] = df["attrition_risk"] * df["revenue_exposure"] total_revenue_risk = df["revenue_risk"].sum() print(f"Total Revenue at Risk: ${total_revenue_risk:,.0f}")
Note: This is an exposure estimate. A more realistic version would use role-weighted exposure or replacement lag costs.
